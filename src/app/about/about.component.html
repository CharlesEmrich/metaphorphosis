What is Metaphorphosis

Metaphorphosis (ˌmedəˈfôrfəsəs) is an exploration into computational linguistics and generative literature.

Simply put, metaphorphosis generates two statements, which may or may not be well-formed and/or non-sensical. The user, then, may select one of the statements. The unselected statement is removed from the page and replaced by a newly generated phrase on the same topic. The user then chooses again, repeating the process for as long as they like.

The statements (originally metaphors) are constructed around a handful of concepts chosen (by human agents) for their complexity, ambiguity, polysemy, or hard-to-define-ness. Hypernyms, hyponyms, and other terms that are semantically or statistically related to the original concept are drawn from the Datamuse API and inserted into grammatical templates creating original statements that may or may not be entertaining or insightful.

If any given statement is chosen a certain number of times consecutively, it is added to the Hall of Fame, where it may be seen and upvoted by other users. From there, clicking on a particular statement takes you to a diagram showing the journey of dichotomies taken to get to that point. It reveals the evolution, the metamorphosis, of the statements.
